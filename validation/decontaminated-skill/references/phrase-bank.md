# Phrase Bank

The exact wording for the moves in SKILL.md. This is a menu, not a script — pick
the phrasing that fits, vary it, don't paste it mechanically. The point is that
each grade and each judgment has a *distinct* register, so the reader can feel
the difference between "I'd bet on this" and "this is a nice guess."

## 1. The evidence-grade ladder

Sort every piece of evidence somewhere on this ladder and *say where*. The whole
value of the exercise is refusing to give everything the same tone.

| Grade | When it applies | English phrasing | Korean phrasing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Decisive / hard-to-fake** | The boring hypotheses genuinely can't survive it | "this is hard-to-fake", "particularly compelling", "this is the cruxy result" | "이건 hard-to-fake evidence다", "가장 결정적", "이게 crux다" |
| **Compelling** | Strong; a gap or two, but it moves me | "more compelling", "this does update me", "fairly persuasive" | "꽤 설득력 있다", "이건 실제로 업데이트가 된다" |
| **Suggestive** | Consistent with the thesis, but soft | "interesting", "suggestive", "worth noting but not load-bearing" | "흥미롭다", "시사적이지만 load-bearing은 아니다" |
| **Mildly interesting** | Real but minor; wouldn't hang anything on it | "mildly interesting", "a nice-to-have", "I don't lean on this" | "약하게 흥미로운 정도", "여기 기대진 않는다" |
| **Confounded / unreliable** | A boring hypothesis fits just as well | "less reliable", "confounded", "cool, but not much evidence" | "덜 믿음직하다", "confound이 있다", "멋지지만 증거는 약하다" |

Note the authentic texture: it's fine to call something "cool but not much
evidence" — praising the *idea* while withholding belief in the *claim* is
exactly the kind of separation this style is built on.

## 2. The three axes

Say all three, and lead with whichever is most surprising. The power move is
decoupling them out loud.

**Importance** (does it matter if true?)
- "The most important claim here is not X, it's Y."
- "Even if I fully bought this, it wouldn't change much — which is why I care
  less about it than the authors seem to."
- "가장 중요한 기여는 방법론 자체가 아니라, 그게 주는 [X]에 대한 증거다."

**Confidence** (do I believe it?)
- "I'm fairly persuaded of X." / "I'm not persuaded of the stronger version."
- "This has updated me." / "I don't buy this yet."
- "이 주장에는 꽤 설득됐다." / "더 강한 버전 Y까지 가지는 않는다."

**Standing** (can I even judge it?)
- "This is the claim I'm least qualified to assess, so I'll hold back."
- "I'm not the right person to evaluate the philosophical framing."
- "이 부분은 내 전문성 밖이라 강한 의견을 내지 않겠다."

The combination that reads as most credible: **high-importance + moderate-
confidence + honest-standing.** "This is the claim that matters most; I'm
persuaded but not certain; and here is exactly why."

## 3. The boring hypothesis

The framing that keeps the analysis honest. Templates:

- "Before I call this [interesting interpretation], the boring version is:
  [mundane mechanism]. What rules that out is [specific result]."
- "A more boring explanation of this result would be D. I don't think the paper
  fully excludes D, which is why I read this result as suggestive, not decisive."
- "The result is consistent with my thesis — but it's *also* consistent with
  [boring hypothesis], so on its own it isn't cruxy."
- "곧바로 [흥미로운 해석]으로 가기 전에, 더 지루한 설명은 [평범한 메커니즘]이다.
  이걸 배제해주는 건 [구체적 결과]다."

The mental discipline: for every result you *like*, produce the boring
hypothesis *before* you produce the exciting one. If you can't state a boring
hypothesis, you probably haven't understood the result well enough to grade it.

## 4. Signature sentence forms

The reasoning collapses into a small number of shapes. All of them share one
structure: **commit, then bound.**

**English**
- "I am persuaded by X, but not by the stronger version Y."
- "The most important claim is not the method itself, but the evidence it gives
  for Z."
- "My best mental model is A. Under A, we should expect B. The paper shows B in
  several hard-to-fake ways."
- "However, result C is less cruxy, because boring hypothesis D could explain it."
- "I expect this to be useful for hypothesis generation, not reliable validation."
- "This is the claim I'm least qualified to judge, so I won't force a view — but
  on the rest, here's where I land."

**Korean**
- "저는 X에는 설득됐지만, 더 강한 버전인 Y까지는 아닙니다."
- "핵심 기여는 방법론 자체가 아니라, 그게 [Z]에 주는 증거라고 봅니다."
- "제 best mental model은 A입니다. A 아래에서라면 B를 기대하게 되는데, 이 연구는
  B를 여러 hard-to-fake한 방식으로 보여줍니다."
- "다만 결과 C는 덜 cruxy합니다 — 더 지루한 가설 D로도 설명되기 때문입니다."
- "이건 hypothesis generation에는 유용하겠지만, 신뢰할 만한 validation 용도는
  아니라고 봅니다."
- "이 주장은 제가 판단할 자격이 가장 부족한 부분이라 강한 의견은 접겠습니다 —
  다만 나머지에 대해서는 이렇게 봅니다."

notes read, and translating them dilutes the meaning. When a Korean gloss helps
